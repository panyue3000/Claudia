Subject: Updated heart age manuscript and MWCCS internal review

Dear all,

Thank you again for taking the time to review the heart age manuscript and send comments. I have revised the AIDS submission version to address the main points raised across the co-author reviews.

The main revisions include softening the interpretation of the CD4 finding, removing speculative language from the abstract and Results, clarifying that this cross-sectional analysis establishes the baseline distribution of PREVENT-derived heart age before future longitudinal work, and de-emphasizing the PTSD findings because PCL-C data were available for only a small subset of participants. I also clarified the rationale for not including individual socioeconomic covariates in the primary models. We did test insurance adjustment in a sensitivity analysis, and the NHB race/ethnicity coefficient was not materially attenuated. Because this was an internal robustness check rather than a primary analysis, I did not add another formal table to the manuscript.

AIDS has a strict word limit, and tables and figures count toward the total. To meet the journal requirements, I also trimmed the paper and combined tables and figures where possible while trying to preserve the main results and interpretation. The limitations section was streamlined, and the MWCCS acknowledgments were updated using the current recommended language.

Following Claudia's additional comments, the abstract Conclusion was rewritten to lead with the HIV-specific findings (serostatus, viral load, and the case for incorporating HIV-specific and psychosocial parameters into future heart-age models). The two PREVENT-in-HIV references she flagged (Durstenfeld 2025 and Grinspoon 2025) were already cited. The final abstract is 250 words and the main text is approximately 3,020 words, both within the AIDS limits.

I am submitting the revised manuscript to the MWCCS internal review committee at the same time. Once it is approved by MWCCS, I will submit it to AIDS. I will keep everyone posted as we move through the final submission process.

Best,
Yue
